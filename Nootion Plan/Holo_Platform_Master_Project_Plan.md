# Holo Platform — Master Project Plan

**Project:** HoloExperience + HoloHost / HoloDesk  
**Company:** Playground Studio  
**Status:** Demo-first, product-minded  
**Primary Goal:** Build compelling, reusable demos that help the sales team and clients understand what is possible, while progressively creating product-ready modules.

---

# 1. Executive Summary

The Holo platform is a family of interactive holographic experiences designed for trade shows, brand activations, product launches, live events, venues, hospitality, retail and permanent installations.

The immediate objective is not to build a perfect software platform.

The immediate objective is to:

1. Create highly understandable demos.
2. Give the sales team clear use cases to sell.
3. Help clients visualize practical applications.
4. Reuse features and modules wherever possible.
5. Gradually evolve successful demos into reusable products.

The platform is divided into two main product families:

## HoloExperience

Interactive holographic experiences for products, brands and live events.

Primary use cases:

- Product Explorer
- Interactive Kiosk
- Live Holographic Connection
- Falso Vivo / Simulated Live Connection
- Brand Experiences
- Product Launch Moments
- Interactive Storytelling

## HoloHost / HoloDesk

AI-powered holographic hosts, brand ambassadors and interactive assistants.

Primary use cases:

- AI Event Host
- AI Brand Ambassador
- AI Concierge
- Airport Assistant
- Hotel / Venue Concierge
- Reception Assistant
- Permanent Information Desk
- Lead Capture Assistant

## 3.5 Falso Vivo

### Internal / Production Name

**Falso Vivo**

### External Description

**Simulated Live Holographic Connection**

### Purpose

Create the experience of a live remote connection using pre-produced content integrated into the event show flow.

### Why It Matters

A false-live workflow can eliminate:

- Connectivity risk
- Speaker availability risk
- Timing mistakes
- Show flow uncertainty

It allows perfect coordination with:

- Lighting
- Audio
- Video
- Stage cues
- Product reveals
- Presenter dialogue
- Event timing

### Example

Host on stage:

> We are now connecting live with our CEO in New York.

The Holobox activates.

The CEO appears and delivers a pre-recorded message designed to feel live and contextual.

### Possible Formats

- Remote executive appearance
- Product launch
- Speaker introduction
- Interview simulation
- Cross-location connection
- Surprise guest
- Award presentation
- Brand character appearance
- Pre-recorded Q&A structure

### Development Requirements

- Playback control
- Cue triggers
- Operator mode
- Timeline / scene control
- Optional scripted interaction
- Emergency stop
- Replay
- Local offline media playback

# 11. Sales Discovery Questions

Sales should ask:

1. What are you trying to show, explain or launch?
2. What should attendees do when they approach the experience?
3. Is there a physical product?
4. Is the product difficult to transport or demonstrate?
5. Is there information hidden inside the product that would be valuable to show?
6. Are there remote speakers or executives involved?
7. Does the experience need to answer questions?
8. Does the experience need to collect information?
9. What should happen after the interaction?
10. Should the experience continue on the guest's phone?

# 12. Development Philosophy

## Demo-First

Build enough to sell.

Do not wait for a perfect platform.

## Product-Minded

Whenever a feature is useful across multiple projects, structure it so it can be reused.

## One Strong Demo Before Many Weak Demos

A polished demo creates more value than several incomplete experiences.

## No Feature Without a Use Case

Every feature should answer:

- What client problem does this solve?
- What user action does this enable?
- What sales conversation does this unlock?

# 25. Key Strategic Risks

## Risk 1 — Selling Hardware Instead of Outcomes

Avoid:

> We rent holographic displays.

Prefer:

> We create interactive holographic experiences.

---

## Risk 2 — Building Too Many Demos

Do not create many weak demonstrations.

Create a small number of strong demos.

---

## Risk 3 — 3D as a Gimmick

A rotating object is not enough.

The experience must reveal, explain, transform, animate, compare or extend.

---

## Risk 4 — Overbuilding the Platform

Do not delay sales waiting for perfect architecture.

Build reusable modules when the use case proves their value.

---

## Risk 5 — Sales Team Confusion

The sales team should not be expected to invent applications.

Give them:

- Clear products
- Clear use cases
- Clear triggers
- Clear discovery questions
- Clear demo scripts
